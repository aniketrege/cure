CuRe: Cultural Gaps in the Long‑Tail of Text‑to‑Image Models

Aniket Rege*1, Zinnia Nie1, Mahesh Ramesh1, Unmesh Raskar1, Zhuoran Yu1, Aditya Kusupati2†, Yong Jae Lee1†, Ramya Korlakai Vinayak1†
1University of Wisconsin‑Madison   2University of Washington
Paper arXiv Code & Dataset

CuRe reveals how state‑of‑the‑art text‑to‑image systems under‑represent the cultural long tail.

Abstract

Popular text‑to‑image (T2I) models are trained on web‑scale data that is heavily Amero‑ and Euro‑centric, leading to hallucinations and mis‑representation for cultures in the global South. We introduce CuRe, a benchmarking suite that diagnoses these cultural gaps.

CuRe couples a hierarchical dataset of 300 cultural artifacts across 64 regions with marginal‑information scorers that better match human judgements of cultural representativeness, image‑text alignment and diversity. Our analysis shows that mainstream T2I systems perform well on head concepts like ceramic diyas but fail on long‑tail artifacts such as jebena or amphora of Hermonax, even when additional prompt detail is provided.
